# Social Media Do's and Don'ts — A Checklist for College Students

---

## ✅ Do's

| # | Do | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Do think before you post.** | Once something is online, it can be screenshotted, shared, and seen by anyone — including future employers. |
| 2 | **Do keep your privacy settings updated.** | Platforms frequently change their privacy policies. Regularly review who can see your posts, profile, and personal information. |
| 3 | **Do use social media to learn and network.** | Follow industry leaders, join professional groups, and engage with educational content on platforms like LinkedIn and Twitter/X. |
| 4 | **Do verify information before sharing it.** | Misinformation spreads fast. Always cross-check facts with reliable sources before reposting news, statistics, or claims. |
| 5 | **Do maintain a professional online image.** | Your digital presence is part of your personal brand. Keep your public-facing profiles clean, respectful, and representative of how you want to be perceived. |

---

## ❌ Don'ts

| # | Don't | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly.** | Avoid posting your phone number, home address, hostel details, college ID, or class schedule where strangers can see them. |
| 2 | **Don't engage in or tolerate cyberbullying.** | If you witness online harassment, report it. Never participate in trolling, body-shaming, or targeted attacks on others. |
| 3 | **Don't accept friend/follow requests from strangers.** | Fake profiles are commonly used for scams, data harvesting, and social engineering attacks. Only connect with people you know or can verify. |
| 4 | **Don't post when you're emotional or angry.** | Impulsive posts often lead to regret. Step away, cool off, and re-read your message before publishing it. |
| 5 | **Don't ignore your digital footprint.** | Google yourself periodically. Old posts, tagged photos, or comments from years ago may not reflect who you are today — clean them up. |

---

*This checklist was created as part of the CSE0001 Digital Literacy Project, VIT Bhopal University.*
