# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online — Prevention Checklist for College Students in India

---

## Actionable Tips to Protect Yourself Online

### 1. 🔐 Use Strong, Unique Passwords for Every Account
- Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters (at least 12 characters long).
- Never reuse the same password across platforms. Use a password manager like Bitwarden (free) to keep track.

### 2. 🔑 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Everywhere
- Turn on 2FA for your college email, GitHub, LinkedIn, Instagram, and banking apps.
- Prefer authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator) over SMS-based OTPs.

### 3. 📱 Never Share Your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with Anyone
- No bank, app, or customer support executive will ever ask for your PIN or OTP.
- If someone calls asking for these, it is a scam — hang up immediately.

### 4. 💸 Understand the Difference Between "Pay" and "Collect" on UPI Apps
- A **collect request** means someone is *requesting money FROM you*, not sending money to you.
- Never approve a collect request from an unknown person. To receive money, you do not need to enter your PIN.

### 5. 🔍 Verify Links Before Clicking
- Hover over links in emails and messages to check the actual URL before clicking.
- Look for `https://` and check the domain name carefully (e.g., `g00gle.com` is NOT Google).
- When in doubt, go directly to the website by typing the URL in your browser.

### 6. 🚫 Do Not Download Apps or Files from Untrusted Sources
- Only install apps from the Google Play Store or Apple App Store.
- Be especially cautious of APK files shared via WhatsApp groups or Telegram channels — they may contain malware or spyware.

### 7. 📧 Learn to Spot Phishing Emails and Messages
- Watch for: urgent language ("Your account will be blocked!"), spelling errors, generic greetings ("Dear Customer"), and suspicious sender addresses.
- If an email claims to be from your bank, verify by calling the bank's official number — not the number in the email.

### 8. 🔒 Keep Your Devices and Apps Updated
- Install software updates as soon as they are available — they often patch critical security vulnerabilities.
- Enable automatic updates on your phone and laptop.

### 9. 🖥️ Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi Networks
- Avoid logging into banking apps, UPI platforms, or email on public/hostel Wi-Fi without a VPN.
- Never enter passwords or financial information on websites that do not use HTTPS.

### 10. 📢 Know Where to Report Cybercrimes

If you are a victim of any cybercrime, report it immediately:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** | [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (toll-free, available 24×7) |
| **Your Bank's Fraud Helpline** | Call immediately to freeze your account if money was stolen |
| **Local Police Cyber Cell** | File an FIR if the amount is significant |

> ⏰ **Time matters:** The sooner you report UPI fraud, the higher the chance of recovering your money. Banks can reverse transactions if reported within 24–48 hours.
