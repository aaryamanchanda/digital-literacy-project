# Task 5 — Cybercrime Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

---

## What Is UPI Payment Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of financial cybercrime where attackers trick victims into authorising unauthorised transactions through popular payment apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, Paytm, or BHIM. Unlike traditional bank fraud that requires account numbers and OTPs, UPI fraud exploits the simplicity of the system — a single click can transfer money instantly.

## How It Typically Happens — A Step-by-Step Scenario

Riya, a second-year college student in Bhopal, listed her old textbooks for sale on an online marketplace. Within hours, she received a call from a man claiming to be an interested buyer. He said he wanted to pay immediately via Google Pay and asked for her UPI ID.

Moments later, Riya received a **"collect request"** on her Google Pay app for ₹1,500 — the listed price. The caller told her, *"I've sent the money, just click Accept to receive it."* Unfamiliar with how UPI collect requests work, Riya entered her PIN and authorised the transaction. Instead of receiving money, ₹1,500 was **debited** from her account.

The scammer then called again, claiming there was a "technical error" and sent another collect request for ₹3,000 — this time saying the refund and original payment would come together. Riya, now suspicious, hung up. But she had already lost ₹1,500.

## Who Is Usually Targeted?

UPI fraud disproportionately targets:
- **College students and young adults** who are digital-first but may not fully understand how UPI payment flows work
- **First-time online sellers** on platforms like OLX, Facebook Marketplace, and Quikr
- **Senior citizens** who use UPI apps but lack awareness of social engineering tactics
- **Small business owners** and delivery workers who process high volumes of digital payments

## Consequences

- **Immediate financial loss** — money transferred via UPI is nearly irreversible
- **Emotional distress** — victims often feel embarrassed and fail to report the crime
- **Privacy breach** — scammers may also request Aadhaar or bank details during the call
- **Repeat victimisation** — scammers share victim lists; once scammed, the same person is often targeted again

## Source

This scenario is based on patterns documented by the **Reserve Bank of India (RBI)** and the **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal** (cybercrime.gov.in). The RBI's 2024 Annual Report noted a 70% rise in UPI-related fraud complaints compared to 2022.
